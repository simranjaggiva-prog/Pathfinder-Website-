Communities & events to join as a student

You don't need a job to be part of the Microsoft tech community. These are all free or low-cost and open to students — great for networking, building your CV, earning free credits, and meeting people who can help you land your first role.

🎓
Microsoft Student AmbassadorsFree

A global student community backed by Microsoft. Host events, earn milestone badges, get mentorship — no application, anyone can join.

 ⚡
Microsoft ReactorFree events

Live and on-demand developer events every week — AI, cloud, DevOps, data and more. All free, all online, run by real Microsoft engineers.

 🏆
Imagine CupCompetition

Microsoft's annual global student competition — build a startup idea using Microsoft tech and compete for cash prizes, mentorship, and a meeting with Satya Nadella.

 🎒
GitHub Student Developer PackFree tools

Free GitHub Pro, Azure credits, domain names and 100+ premium dev tools — just sign up with your university email address. Worth hundreds of dollars.

 ☁️
Azure for StudentsFree credits

$100 USD in free Azure cloud credits and access to 25+ free services — no credit card needed. Spin up real cloud apps while you're still studying.

 💬
Microsoft Tech CommunityForum

Ask questions, read expert blogs and join discussions on Azure, AI, Power Platform, Security and everything Microsoft. Great for staying up to date.

 🤝
Azure & Microsoft MeetupsLocal events

Find local Azure and Microsoft tech meetups in your city. Real people, real networking — a great way to meet engineers and hear about job openings.

 🚀
Microsoft Learn Student HubResources

The official student landing page on Microsoft Learn — curated paths, free open-source curricula on GitHub, Imagine Cup info and Azure for Students, all in one spot.
Women in tech — communities, programmes & support

Only around 26% of tech roles are held by women — but that's changing, and you can be part of the shift. These communities, scholarships and Microsoft programmes are here to support you every step of the way.

👩‍💻
The tech industry needs you. Women who work in tech report higher job satisfaction, stronger salaries and incredible career progression. These resources are designed to make that path more accessible.

💜
Women Who CodeFree

Global community of 300,000+ women in tech. Free membership gives you access to events, a jobs board, study groups, and a network of engineers at top companies.

 🌟
AnitaB.org & Grace HopperCommunity

Home of the Grace Hopper Celebration — the world's largest gathering of women technologists. AnitaB.org also has scholarships, mentorship and career programmes.

 🚀
Microsoft LEAPMicrosoft

Microsoft's apprenticeship programme designed for people from non-traditional backgrounds — including a strong focus on women returning to or entering tech. Paid and leads to full-time roles.

 🎓
NCWITScholarships

National Center for Women & IT — offers the Aspirations in Computing Award and connects women with scholarships, internships and employers committed to diversity in tech.

 🏢
Microsoft Careers for StudentsMicrosoft

Microsoft's student internship and new-grad programmes — including dedicated pathways and scholarships for underrepresented groups. Apply for internships and explore early-in-career roles.

 ☁️
Women in CloudFree

Global community dedicated to the economic empowerment of women through cloud technology. Events, mentorship, job opportunities and career programmes — perfect if you're heading into Azure, DevOps or data engineering.

 🚧
Code Without BarriersMicrosoft

Microsoft's flagship initiative for women in tech — hackathons, mentorship programmes and community events that actively break down the barriers stopping women from entering and thriving in technology careers.

You don't need a CS degree, a perfect background, or to have always known you wanted to work in tech. These stories reflect the kinds of paths women take into Microsoft tech roles every day.

Power Platform Developer — career change from HR

WiCyS gave me my first security mentor. I earned SC-900, then SC-200, and now I work in a SOC. Two years ago I didn't know what a firewall was.
